The Sacred Kedarnath Pilgrimage

Kedarnath is one of the most revered pilgrimage sites in India, housing one of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. Situated at an altitude of 3,583 meters in the Garhwal Himalayas, the ancient temple stands as a testament to unwavering faith and divine grace.

According to Hindu mythology, this is where the Pandavas sought Lord Shiva's blessings after the Mahabharata war. The temple, believed to be over 1,000 years old, survived the devastating 2013 floods ‚Äì a miracle attributed to divine protection.

About Kedarnath Temple

Discover the divine significance of this ancient shrine

Kedarnath Temple

Rudraprayag District

The Kedarnath temple is built of large, heavy stone slabs over a rectangular platform. The temple has a conical lingam inside the sanctum, which is irregular in shape. The temple complex includes the Adi Shankaracharya samadhi and is surrounded by snow-capped peaks including Kedarnath peak.

Your Journey Route

A day-by-day overview of the Kedarnath Yatra itinerary

1

Day 1: Arrival in Haridwar

Haridwar

Arrive at Haridwar, attend the famous Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri. Check into hotel and prepare for the journey ahead.

2

Day 2: Haridwar to Guptkashi

Guptkashi

Drive to Guptkashi (220km) through scenic mountain roads. Visit Ardh Narishwar Temple. Overnight stay and prepare for the trek.

220km drive
3

Day 3: Guptkashi to Kedarnath

Kedarnath

Drive to Gaurikund (30km). Begin the 18km trek to Kedarnath (or take helicopter). Reach temple, attend evening aarti. Overnight at Kedarnath.

30km drive + 18km trek
4

Day 4: Kedarnath to Haridwar

Haridwar

Early morning darshan. Trek back to Gaurikund and drive to Haridwar. Attend evening Ganga Aarti. Departure.

Return journey
