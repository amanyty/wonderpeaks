The Sacred Pilgrimage Tradition

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our Yatra services

Char Dham Yatra covers all four sacred shrines - Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath - requiring 10-12 days. Do Dham focuses on Kedarnath and Badrinath only, taking 5-6 days, ideal for pilgrims with limited time. Both offer profound spiritual experiences, but Char Dham provides the complete pilgrimage circuit.
The best time is May to June and September to October. The temples open in April/May after winter and close by November due to heavy snowfall. Monsoon season (July-August) should be avoided due to landslide risks. We recommend booking in advance during peak season.
Panch Kedar is a pilgrimage to five sacred Shiva temples - Kedarnath, Tungnath, Rudranath, Madhyamaheshwar, and Kalpeshwar. According to mythology, these temples represent different body parts of Lord Shiva. The complete circuit requires good physical fitness as it involves trekking to remote Himalayan locations.
Yes, helicopter services are available from Phata, Guptkashi, and Sirsi helipads to Kedarnath. This is ideal for elderly pilgrims or those with physical limitations. We can arrange priority helicopter bookings as part of your package. Weather conditions may affect helicopter operations.

Some packages also include helicopter services, porter/pony arrangements, and special puja arrangements. Contact us for customized packages.
